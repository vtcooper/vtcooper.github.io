PhD Student in Climate Dynamics

I am an Atmospheric Sciences PhD student and National Defense Science & Engineering Graduate (NDSEG) Fellow at the University of Washington.

My current research at UW focuses on coupled climate dynamics and data assimilation, e.g., how spatial patterns of sea-surface temperature impact radiative feedbacks and climate sensitivity inferred from paleoclimate record. I am advised by Kyle Armour, Cecilia Bitz, and Greg Hakim.

I am originally from Granville, a small town in rural Ohio. Before UW, I studied at Harvard (major in Statistics) then worked in New York City as an investment banking analyst and private equity associate.
