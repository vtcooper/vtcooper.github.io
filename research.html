Research

I study coupled climate dynamics focused on atmosphere-ocean-ice interactions, radiative feedbacks, and climate variability. I am currently investigating how climate feedbacks and Earth's energy budget changed in the past (in the Last Glacial Maximum and recent instrumental records) due to variations in the spatial patterns of sea-surface temperature (SST). This work helps constrain what could happen in the future as greenhouse gases warm the planet.

I run experiments in global climate models, I use statistical methods (data assimilation and linear inverse models) to analyze past climate periods, and I analyze observations to improve understanding of climate dynamics. Data assimilation plays a major role in my research because it is the statistical tool that enables reconstruction of past SST patterns with sparse observations. Polar climate and the role of ice has been a theme in my search in both my study of the Last Glacial Maximum (~20,000 years ago) and in my research on interactions between ocean surface waves and sea ice.
