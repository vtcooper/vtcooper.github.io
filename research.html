Research

I study coupled climate dynamics, including atmosphere-ocean-ice interactions, radiative feedbacks, and climate variability. I am currently investigating how climate feedbacks and Earth's energy budget have changed in the past, specifically over the Last Glacial Maximum and recent instrumental records, and I am focusing on the role of spatial patterns of sea-surface temperature (SST) in determining the global radiative feedback.

I run experiments in global climate models, I use statistical methods (data assimilation and linear inverse models) to analyze past climate periods, and I analyze observations to improve understanding of climate dynamics. Data assimilation plays a key role in my research because it is the statistical tool that enables reconstruction of past SST patterns with sparse observations. Polar climate and the role of ice has been a theme through my study of the Last Glacial Maximum (~20,000 years ago) and my work on interactions between ocean surface waves and sea ice.

SST Pattern Effect in the Paleoclimate Record

This work focuses on how the spatial pattern of sea-surface temperature (SST) impacts climate feedbacks and sensitivity. We have found that the spatial pattern of cooling during the Last Glacial Maximum (LGM) is associated with a more positive feedback, compared to the pattern of warming we expect from a doubling of carbon dioxide (CO2). If we use the LGM as a constraint on the climate sensitivity that is relevant to modern-day global warming, we need to make an adjustment for how these different SST patterns drive different radiative feedbacks (and climate sensitivities). This adjustment reduces estimates of future warming and helps constrain what could happen in the future as greenhouse gases warm the planet. Additional planned work will explore SST pattern effects in the Pliocene (a past warm period ~3 million years ago) and the recent instrumental record (1850-present). See my most recent poster presented at CFMIP in July 2022 [Download Poster].

Collaborators: Kyle Armour, Cristi Proistosescu, Greg Hakim, Yue Dong, Jessica Tierney, Matthew Osman, Natalie Burls, Daniel Amrhein, Timothy Andrews, and Yi Ming.
